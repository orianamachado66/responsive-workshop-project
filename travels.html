Dia de Muertos - Mexico

As October comes to an end, Mexico bursts into a riot of colors and flavors! Dia de los Muertos, also known as the Day of the Dead, isn't a somber occasion; it's a vibrant celebration of life and death. Families come together to remember and honor their loved ones who have passed away. It's a joyful reunion, not a goodbye.
You'll spot beautifully created altars popping up all over the place, from the bustling streets to cozy family homes. These are like love letters to tradition, decked out with the bright and sunny marigold flowers, known as "cempas√∫chil" in Mexico - trust me, they've got twenty petals of pure awesomeness!

Mallorca Island - Spain

Mallorca, Spain, is a stunning Mediterranean gem that promises an unforgettable escape. Immerse yourself in crystal-clear waters, explore picturesque villages, and savor delectable Spanish cuisine. From charming local markets to historic castles, Mallorca offers a diverse and authentic island experience.

PS: The beaches here are nothing short of paradise. With powdery sands and waters so clear you can count the fish, you'll quickly realize that 'beach day' in Mallorca means 'every day.'

Puebla - Mexico

Puebla, Mexico, is a delightful blend of rich traditions and mouthwatering dishes. Experience the famous mole poblano, wander through historic streets, and join lively celebrations. Savor authentic mariachi music, and let Puebla's charm captivate you.


Tip: Explore local crafts and discover the art of Talavera pottery. These exquisite hand-painted ceramics are not just souvenirs; they're works of art that capture Puebla's artistic soul.
